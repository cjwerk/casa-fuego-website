Our Story

Meet Milo Herrera

Milo Herrera grew up in a kitchen that was always full of noise, color, and the irresistible smell of chilis toasting on a comal. Born in San Antonio to parents who had immigrated from Santa Anna, El Salvador, Milo learned to cook at the side of his grandmother, Abuela Rosario, who believed that food was the language the heart speaks when words aren't enough. By the time Milo was twelve, he could make mole negro from memory — all twenty-one ingredients, in the right order, stirred with the same patience his grandmother had shown him. It was Abuela Rosario who first said, "Mijo, one day you'll feed the whole city." Milo never forgot those words.

After earning a culinary degree from the Culinary Institute of San Antonio and spending five years cooking in restaurants across Texas and Mexico City, Milo returned home with a clear vision: open a restaurant that felt like family. In 2011, he opened the doors of Papa Milos in a converted bungalow on Riverwalk Boulevard. The restaurant was small, just fourteen tables, but the food was extraordinary. Word spread quickly, and within a year, there was a line out the door every weekend. Today, Papa Milos seats over eighty guests, has been featured in Texas Monthly and the San Antonio Express-News, and has won the city's "Best El Salvadoran Restaurant" award four years running. Milo still cooks five nights a week, because, as he says, "The day I stop cooking is the day it stops being Papa Milos."

What Makes Papa Milos Unique

Papa Milos is not a chain restaurant, and it never will be. Every recipe served here began in a home kitchen — either Abuela Rosario's in Oaxaca, or Milo's own family table. Our mole negro takes three days to prepare. Our birria is braised for twelve hours. Our tortillas are pressed and cooked to order, one at a time. We don't use shortcuts, and we don't believe in "close enough." When you eat at Papa Milos, you're tasting cooking that has been refined across three generations and thousands of hours of practice.

We are also deeply committed to our community. We source produce weekly from local San Antonio farms, partner with the San Antonio Food Bank to donate leftover food every night, and offer a paid apprenticeship program for culinary students from local community colleges. For Milo, Papa Milos has always been about more than the food — it's about building something lasting, and sharing it with everyone.
