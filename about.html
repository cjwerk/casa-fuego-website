Our Story

Meet Elena Herrera

Elena Herrera grew up in a kitchen that was always full of noise, color, and the irresistible smell of chilis toasting on a comal. Born in San Antonio to parents who had immigrated from Oaxaca, Mexico, Elena learned to cook at the side of her grandmother, Abuela Rosario, who believed that food was the language the heart speaks when words aren't enough. By the time Elena was twelve, she could make mole negro from memory — all twenty-one ingredients, in the right order, stirred with the same patience her grandmother had shown her. It was Abuela Rosario who first said, "Mija, one day you'll feed the whole city." Elena never forgot those words.

After earning a culinary degree from the Culinary Institute of San Antonio and spending five years cooking in restaurants across Texas and Mexico City, Elena returned home with a clear vision: open a restaurant that felt like family. In 2011, she opened the doors of Casa Fuego — "House of Fire" — in a converted bungalow on Riverwalk Boulevard. The restaurant was small, just fourteen tables, but the food was extraordinary. Word spread quickly, and within a year, there was a line out the door every weekend. Today, Casa Fuego seats over eighty guests, has been featured in Texas Monthly and the San Antonio Express-News, and has won the city's "Best Mexican Restaurant" award four years running. Elena still cooks five nights a week, because, as she says, "The day I stop cooking is the day it stops being Casa Fuego."

What Makes Casa Fuego Unique

Casa Fuego is not a chain restaurant, and it never will be. Every recipe served here began in a home kitchen — either Abuela Rosario's in Oaxaca, or Elena's own family table. Our mole negro takes three days to prepare. Our birria is braised for twelve hours. Our tortillas are pressed and cooked to order, one at a time. We don't use shortcuts, and we don't believe in "close enough." When you eat at Casa Fuego, you're tasting cooking that has been refined across three generations and thousands of hours of practice.

We are also deeply committed to our community. We source produce weekly from local San Antonio farms, partner with the San Antonio Food Bank to donate leftover food every night, and offer a paid apprenticeship program for culinary students from local community colleges. For Elena, Casa Fuego has always been about more than the food — it's about building something lasting, and sharing it with everyone.
